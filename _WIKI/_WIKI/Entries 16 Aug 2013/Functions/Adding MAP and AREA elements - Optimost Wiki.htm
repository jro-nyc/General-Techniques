The issue

The IE issues is that you need to use "useMap" as the attribute with a capital "M". Other than that, the same.

Creating and adding Map/Area tags with an image

Hers is the HTML for it: 

<img src="http://lomalindahealth.org/media/images/optimost-homepage-b2.jpg" usemap="#jaimito" />
<map name="jaimito">
	<area coords="210,12,700,95" href="http://www.healing.com" />
	<area coords="292,109,600,159" href="http://www.perspectives.com" />
</map>

Through the DOM, you say: 

var _img1=document.createElement('img');
_img1.setAttribute('border','0');
_img1.setAttribute('useMap','#jaimito');
_img1.setAttribute('src','http://lomalindahealth.org/media/images/optimost-homepage-b2.jpg');

_map=document.createElement('map');
_area1=document.createElement('area');
_area2=document.createElement('area');
_map.setAttribute('id',"jaimito");
_map.setAttribute('NAME',"jaimito");
_area1.href='http://www.healing.com';
_area1.shape="rect";
_area1.coords='210,12,700,95';
_area2.href='http://www.perspectives.com';
_area2.shape="rect";
_area2.coords='292,109,600,159';

_map.appendChild(_area1);
_map.appendChild(_area2);
document.body.appendChild(_map);
document.body.appendChild(_img1);

Do I have to add the MAP and AREA elements through the DOM?

On a DOM template, what are you talking about, er, of course...... NOT. In a DOM template, you have to access page elements through the DOM, change them, modify them, manipulate them. However, since the MAP and AREA statements ARE NOT VISIBLE, you can in fact just add the HTML to the Optimost code, and it will be rendered in a document.write, and then enter the DOM and work as normal. So remember that you can do it either way.
