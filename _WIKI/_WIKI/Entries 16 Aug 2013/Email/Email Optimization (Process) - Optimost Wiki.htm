Introduction

This page describes the process of optimizing email from a Client Services/project management perspective. For an in-depth description of technical implementation issues, see Email Optimization (Implementation).

Email marketing is a powerful marketing tool that allows companies to:

  • turn prospects into customers
  • build solid relationships with existing customers
  • foster brand loyalty and repeat business
  • minimize impact of Shopping Cart abandonment (send email to prospects that abandon shopping cart)
  • Provide product news, helpful tips, special offers, coupons, upsells and crosssells, gain feedback.


It has the following business benefits:

  • Cost-effective and high ROI ($US51.45 return for every marketing dollar spent) (Source:Direct Marketing Association)
  • Effective in generating product interest (62% visit a Web site when they receive an e-mail promotion)(Source:Decision Direct Research)
  • Immediate, measurable results
  • Broad reach (viral marketing)

How is email marketing managed (org roles)?

Business owners varies by client (Product Marketing, Direct Marketing, Online Marketing).

Typically, the owner is different than our day-to-day contact. So if we want to build value for the client we should build a relationship with the email marketing owner.

What are clients looking to improve?

  • Open rates
  • Clickthru rates
  • Conversion rates

Implementation

Launching and data-processing is specific to email. There are two implementation methods, integrated and non-integrated.

Tracking

  • No cookies. No JavaScript within email, so we can't drop a cookie. Client Website will need to look for session identifier in the query string of the URL.
  • Open rates
  • Clickthrus Clickthrus fired links, so Optimost was able to drop a cookie after the link had been clicked based on a query string appended to the landing page URL (full integration), so that you can distinguish email hits from search hits. You can then track the user downstream.

Dynamic Email

"Dynamic email" is email which has certain content elements that change depending on who the email is going to and the subject matter of the email. Some dynamic emails are simple (e.g., Dear $CustomerName) some are more complex (Live Nation email with Band concert dates, includes 4 plus dynamic components.)

Dynamic emails are tricky because we can't use JavaScript within an email.

To deal with this:

  • In STP, identify the dynamic areas.
  • When Optimost delivers the email HTML to the client, there will be placeholder tags the client needs to populate with real data and then load the email html into their email application then send out.
  • For simple dynamic emails, the client's email application may be able to automatically populate the dynamic content.

A customer solution for complex dynamic email was developed for Live Nation: Jim developed an application for the client that would take the band and creative number, call to an Optimost template, and the Optimost would generate an HTML template with placeholder tags that would retrieve XML information from the client's servers, then delivered the final HTML to the client's servers for delivery.

See Jim or Dave Gaskins for more details on this solution.
