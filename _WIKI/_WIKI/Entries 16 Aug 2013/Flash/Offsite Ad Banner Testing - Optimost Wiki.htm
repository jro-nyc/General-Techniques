Optimizing Off-Site Banner Advertisements

Clients invest a large percentage of their marketing dollars on off-site Flash banner advertising in an effort to bring visitors to their Web site. Sometimes this can equate to millions of dollars and optimization of those banners could greatly help increase the return on investment.


How Does It Work?
Optimost can serve XML as well as it serves HTML and CSS. The template has to be defined differently, but there are no Optimost-specific limitations to using XML as your served product versus HTML. When using XML, it is not served directly into the web page as a parser is needed to use the XML data. For these experiments, we either:

  1. Serve the XML to the customer's Server, and the client generates the appropriate HTML.

  2. Serve the XML to a Flash movie in a web browser that then modifies the flash movie based on the XML data.

Flash has the capability to load data using XML, and Optimost can take advantage of this functionality. The Flash movie uses ActionScript code that will make the call to the Optimost servers, which responds with XML data defining the experiment's values, and the Action script code will then take appropriate action based on those values. The data passed in is usually either images (URLs), or "instruction" to tell the movie what to play.

The benefit of this implementation is that the ad server (i.e. Doubleclick, Atlas, etc.) is agnostic. Meaning, Optimost’s implementation works independently of ad servers so it can be used regardless of vendor used for serving the advertisements.


What is the Recommended Strategy to Setup the Test?

Once the client has identified the banner for optimization, begin working through a test plan with the client and their agency if appropriate. Limit the number of variables to 3, and no more than 2 values each; the smaller the better.

The more of a partner you can become with a client’s agency the better chance Optimost has of working with the agency on other MVT projects whether with this client or a different client that they agency can introduce us to.

Try to encourage your client to use three or fewer banner sizes. This will be less work on their Flash Developer and less overhead for the Optimost analysts to manage. Each advertisement will require its own subject.

Because there will be a lot of traffic pushed through these personas, be prepared to move the personas to a .xx wave each time it reaches 1,000,000 views.

Is My Client a Good Candidate for Off-Site Banner Advertising?

There are some inherent challenges with optimizing off-site banner advertising because the clickthru rate is so low that a client needs a large amount of traffic. This type of test isn’t appropriate for a client that isn’t heavily investing large sums of money into this medium.

With an industry standard of a .05% clickthru rate or less, this type of test can take months to complete.

In the case of Wachovia, their 3 variable 2 value experiment has taken over 3 months to complete even though they had nearly 15 million views. Whoever your client is, they need to be committed to multi-variable testing on their banners and be able to run the campaign for a long period of time if they are to get any results.

Wachovia chose to only test 33% of their traffic over a 3-4 month timeframe. Even though the test stayed in wave 1 for a long period of time, it was critical for the success of the experiment to have a meeting with the agency and the client every 2-3 weeks. Invite the Optimost analyst to these meetings and be prepared to show reports and progress. Without these checkpoints, a client will wonder what is taking so long and whether you are keeping track of their test.

At the conclusion of the experiment, it is also important to formally summarize the results of the test even if significance hasn’t been reached. This way the client understands that we are waiting on business decisions from them.
